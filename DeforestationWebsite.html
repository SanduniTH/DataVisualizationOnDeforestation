DEFORESTATION

Deforestation is the permanent removal of trees to make room for something besides forest. This can include clearing the land for agriculture or grazing, or using the timber for fuel, construction or manufacturing.

According to the above graph we can see that the country that is losing the most Tropical Primary Rainforest in 2018 is Brazil. It has lost 1,1347,132 ha. Therefore, it is visible that the world is losing a higher amount of Tropical Primary Rainforest than we are aware of. People should focus their attention towards this and take a step to minimise deforestation.

As we can see that most countries have reduced their forest cover in the last 15 years. If we focus on Brazil, in 2000 there was a forest cover of 62.37%. But in the year 2015, it was reduced to 59.05%. Most countries have at least reduced their forest cover in 1%. While some countries have worked on reforestation and have increased their forest cover. An example of such a country is China. In the year 2000, China only had 18.78% of forest cover. But after 15 years, they have increased their forest cover up to 22.1%. We should also learn from Countries such as China and reduce deforestation and focus on reforestation.
